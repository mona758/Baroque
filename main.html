The Baroque Toolkit: How the Drama was Made

1. Chiaroscuro (The Light & The Dark)

Baroque artists were the first "cinematographers." They used a single, harsh light source to create deep shadows and blinding highlights.

2. The Diagonals (The Energy)

While Renaissance art was built on stable triangles and squares, Baroque art lives on diagonals.

3. The "In-Your-Face" Perspective

Baroque art breaks the "fourth wall."
The Trick: Objects (like a foot, a hand, or a table edge) are often painted so they seem to stick out toward you, making you feel like you are standing inside the scene.
